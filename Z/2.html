Pamalabas Ya Gawat (PAGA) project reached 725 households across Santa Barbara Pangasinan

by Mauro Nepomuceno

The Pamalabas Ya Gawat (PAGA) Project extended aid to 725 households across Santa Barbara, Pangasinan, delivering essential support to families in need.

The Pamalabas-na-Gawat (PAGA) Project, initiated by the Santa Barbara Pangasinan Association of America, Inc. (SBPAAI), is a vital civic initiative aimed at providing food aid to the town's most vulnerable residents. Despite inclement weather and an impending typhoon, 25 beneficiaries braved the rains to collect their food packages at Daroy Elementary School in Brgy. ERFE. Under the dedicated leadership of Mdm. Ramelita Patani-DeGuzman Tarlit, the project continues to bring essential support to households living in dire conditions along the Sinucalan River and other underserved areas. The initiative seeks to prevent the emergence of a culture of mendacity by offering a hand up to those in need.

The success of PAGA relies heavily on the collaboration of local volunteers, including teachers, scholars, and community leaders. Teachers from Minien-Tebag Elementary School worked together to distribute food packages to their barangays, while iCAN Foundation scholars and volunteers like Fatima Pantil and Maloney Manuel Quinto took responsibility for transporting aid to more remote areas like Dalongue. Mdm. Ramie, alongside her husband, helped distribute 125 food bags to multiple barangays, including Maningding, Leet, and Maticmatic, proving the power of collective effort in addressing the needs of the community. The project also highlights the importance of local leadership and partnerships. Dalongue Brgy. Capt. Angel Tamayo’s commitment to transporting the 725 food packages across the town’s 29 barangays ensured the distribution process ran smoothly. Longtime volunteers such as Mdm. Herminia Villafania Quiambao and Mdm. Myrna Bandong Delin also made significant contributions, working to cover areas without assigned coordinators. Additionally, the support from Mdm. Jesellete Desamiro and Jmacks Resort & Hotel was crucial, providing a venue for repacking and meals for volunteers, further demonstrating the community’s solidarity.

While the Pamalabas-na-Gawat Project started as a modest endeavor, it has become an important lifeline for many families in Santa Barbara. With the continued support of SBPAAI’s members, their families, and generous donors, the project aims to grow and reach even more households in need. As this initiative continues, SBPAAI hopes to inspire more people to join in the effort, ensuring that Santa Barbara’s most vulnerable citizens are not forgotten, even during the toughest times.
