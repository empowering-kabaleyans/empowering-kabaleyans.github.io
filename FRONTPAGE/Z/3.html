SBPAAI Scholars Get Together Year End Party

by Mauro Nepomuceno

The Scholars' Year-End Get-Together 2024 took place on December 27, 2024, in the vibrant setting of Jmacks Garden, celebrating education, unity, and shared achievements.

On December 27th, the Santa Barbara Pangasinan Association of America, Inc. (SBPAAI) hosted a truly memorable event at Jmacks Garden, bringing together 37 hardworking scholars from the Nieves Fernandez CSP and iCAN Foundation. This annual celebration was a vibrant gathering of individuals who have shown incredible dedication and passion for their education, and it was a day filled with joy, laughter, and gratitude. The event was generously organized by Ma'am Lette Desa, and the highlight of the day was undoubtedly the buffet spread, which, at only 599 PHP per person, proved to be an absolute steal. Although some of the scholars might have been recovering from a recent detox (we all know how post-holiday purging can go!), everyone was more than ready to indulge in the delicious array of food. While lechon (roast pig) wasn’t featured this year, attendees are already hoping for its return next year, ensuring that there will be something to look forward to in future celebrations.

The real magic of the event, however, was in the sense of community and connection it fostered. Beyond the food, the day was a celebration of the scholars’ hard work and achievements. The atmosphere was filled with excitement as the participants engaged in games, bonding activities, and casual conversations, strengthening the ties that unite them. For these scholars, supported by the Nieves Fernandez CSP and iCAN Foundation, the event was a reminder of their resilience and the importance of their shared journey. It wasn’t just about celebrating the holidays; it was about honoring the dedication these young individuals bring to their studies and to their community.

As the event came to a close, the sense of camaraderie and gratitude lingered in the air. This gathering has become a cherished tradition, thanks to the leadership of SBPAAI President Sal Calaguio and the organizing team, who work tirelessly to make this annual event a reality. Their dedication ensures that the spirit of celebration and community continues to thrive year after year. A heartfelt thank you goes out to all the volunteers, donors, and everyone who contributed to making the day such a success. The scholars left with not only full stomachs but also a renewed sense of motivation, friendship, and a deeper connection to the people who support them. Everyone is already looking forward to next year’s event, where we can only hope that the lechon will finally make its highly anticipated return.
